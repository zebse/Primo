Our Services

Seeds

Seeds represent one of the core commodities traded by Primo Trading House. This includes various types of seeds used for agricultural purposes, such as crop cultivation or planting, catering to both domestic and international markets.

Beans

Beans are another key commodity handled by Primo Trading House, encompassing a variety of legumes such as soybeans, lentils, and kidney beans. These versatile crops are widely consumed worldwide and play a significant role in global food security and agricultural trade

Peas

Peas are a significant agricultural product traded by Primo Trading House, encompassing various types such as green peas, yellow peas, and split peas. These legumes are valued for their nutritional content and versatility in culinary applications, making them a staple in diets worldwide.

Sunflower

Sunflower is a vital commodity in Primo Trading House's portfolio, comprising both seeds and oil. The company engages in the export and import of sunflower seeds for cultivation and processing, as well as sunflower oil for culinary and industrial uses. Sunflower products hold significant market demand globally, contributing to the company's diversified revenue streams.

Cereal

Cereals are grasses cultivated for their edible grains and are the world's largest crops, making them staple foods globally. They are grown in vast quantities and provide more food energy than any other crop type. In Ethiopia, a wide variety of cereals are cultivated, including teff, maize, wheat, barley, sorghum, millet, oats, and rice.

Elephant Grass

Elephant grass or Napier grass is an important crop that provides good fodder for grazing animals and dairy farmers. Being an important food item for African elephants, it’s named ‘Elephant grass’. The grass is rough, hairy, green, yellow or purple-coloured that can grow ~15 feet tall. The leaves are narrow and have a white central vein, pointed tips, sparse hairs and razor-sharp edges that are impenetrable. The grass has multi-branched stems that often grow in dense, bamboo-like clumps.

Quality Assurance

Cleaning Process.

Control of the raw sesame is initiated right from the farm level to ensure that all raw materials are only packaged in clean polypropylene bags. Clean trucks are used and the trucks are not opened during transport.

Sorting Process

Samples are drawn which are sent for checking for residue analysis and the raw material is also checked for moisture level, purity etc. The raw material from the warehouse stored in lots is divided into batches.

De-Hulling Process

The cleaned sesame is emptied into the peeler tank, which has a capacity of 19 MT. All machines are linked together using pneumatic System. which scoop the seeds, thereby avoiding breaking of seeds during movements from one machine to another.

Roasting Process

The next stage in the hulling process makes use of an air filter that filters air into a blower, with the air proceeding into the heater, where the air is heated and transported into the dryer.
