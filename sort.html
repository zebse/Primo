Sorted Products

Sorting separates foods based on a single measurable property like size, weight, or color. Grading assesses overall quality using multiple attributes and properties. seed can be hand-sorted to remove discoloured seed, thereby reducing the level of contamination by certain seed-borne fungi and improving seed germination

SESAME SEEDS

The company is an exporter specializing in high-quality hulled and unhulled sesame seeds for international markets. The hulled seeds undergo careful processing to remove the outer husk, resulting in a lighter color.

SOYBEANS

Soybeans are a legume crop that are rich in protein, fiber, and various nutrients. They are widely used in a variety of food products, including tofu, soy milk, and edamame, as well as animal feed.

WHITE BEANS

White beans, a versatile pantry staple, come in various shapes and sizes. They offer a mild, creamy texture and subtle flavor, making them perfect for soups, stews, dips, and creamy casseroles.

RED KIDNEY BEANS

Red kidney beans, named for their resemblance to a red kidney, are a powerhouse legume. They boast a hearty texture, earthy flavor, and rich nutritional profile, making them a delicious and healthy addition to chili, stews, and various international dishes.

RICE

Rice is a cereal grain and in its domesticated form is the staple food of over half of the world's population, particularly in Asia and Africa. Rice is the seed of the grass species Oryza sativa (Asian rice)—or, much less commonly, Oryza glaberrima (African rice).

MAIZE

Maize is one of the major food crops in Ethiopia and plays an important role in the livelihood of the farming community. The nutritive value of common maize is quite similar to other cereal grains characterized with low protein quality and high carbohydrate content.

OATS

Oats, formally named Avena sativa, is a type of cereal grain from the Poaceae grass family of plants.Mechanically or optically sorted oats are sorted by a combination of machines and humans to remove errant gluten-containing grains.

BLACK EYED PEAS

Black-eyed peas, sometimes called cowpeas, are a popular legume enjoyed worldwide. They offer a mild, slightly sweet flavor and a pleasing firm texture with a signature black eye in the center. These versatile beans are a staple in Southern cuisine and many other cultures, often enjoyed in stews, soups, and rice dishes.

CRANBERRY BEANS

Cranberry beans, known for their beautiful speckled appearance that fades when cooked, offer a creamy texture and a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. These versatile beans are a favorite in Italian dishes, soups, and stews, bringing a delicious and visually interesting element to your meals.

BLACK KIDNEY BEANS

Black beans, boasting a deep, rich flavor and meaty texture, are a versatile pantry staple. They add a satisfying heartiness to soups, salads, dips, and countless other dishes.
