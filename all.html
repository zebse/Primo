Agro Processing

Oil Seeds

Oilseeds are crucial commodities in agro-processing industries, serving as primary sources for extracting edible oils and valuable by-products. Some prominent oilseeds in agro-processing include SOYABEANS, SUNFLOWER seeds, PEANUT (Groundnut), COTTONSEEDS, PALM KERNELS, SESAME seeds, NIGER seed, and FLAXSEEDS. Each oilseed possesses unique characteristics in terms of oil content, nutritional composition, and processing requirements, influencing their utilization in food, feed, and industrial applications.

Pulses

Consider pulses seeds – the nutritional powerhouses that are in high demand worldwide! Pulses seeds, including LENTILS, CHICKPEAS, BEANS (e.g. kidney beans, black beans, pinto beans, etc.), Mung beans, and PEAS, are not only delicious but also packed with protein, fiber, and essential nutrients. Our export company specializes in sourcing premium-quality pulses seeds from trusted growers around the globe, ensuring consistent quality and reliability for our clients.

Cleaned Products

NIGER SEED

Niger seeds, also known as black sesame seeds, are tiny, oil-rich seeds with a nutty flavor. They add a delightful crunch and a touch of visual interest to baked goods, salads, and various dishes.

SESAME SEEDS

The company is an exporter specializing in high-quality hulled and unhulled sesame seeds for international markets. The hulled seeds undergo careful processing to remove the outer husk, resulting in a lighter color.

SOYBEANS

Soybeans are a legume crop that are rich in protein, fiber, and various nutrients. They are widely used in a variety of food products, including tofu, soy milk, and edamame, as well as animal feed.

PEAS

Peas are small, round green legumes that grow in pods. They are a common vegetable used in many dishes, offering a sweet, slightly starchy flavor, and providing a good source of fiber, vitamins, and minerals.

PEANUTS

Peanuts are a type of legume that grow underground, producing pods that contain edible seeds inside. They are a popular snack food and cooking ingredient, and are a rich source of protein, healthy fats, and various vitamins and minerals.

LENTIL

Lentils are small, lens-shaped legumes that are inexpensive, highly nutritious, and commonly used in a variety of savory dishes. They come in different varieties based on color, size, and flavor, and are valued for their high protein, fiber, and mineral content.

WHITE BEANS

White beans, a versatile pantry staple, come in various shapes and sizes. They offer a mild, creamy texture and subtle flavor, making them perfect for soups, stews, dips, and creamy casseroles.

MUG BEANS

Mug beans, also known as moong dal, are tiny, green lentils prized for their quick cooking time and subtle, slightly sweet flavor. They're a dietary staple in many cultures, enjoyed in soups, curries, and even sprouted as a nutritious addition to salads.

RED KIDNEY BEANS

Red kidney beans, named for their resemblance to a red kidney, are a powerhouse legume. They boast a hearty texture, earthy flavor, and rich nutritional profile, making them a delicious and healthy addition to chili, stews, and various international dishes.

BLACK KIDNEY BEANS

Black beans, boasting a deep, rich flavor and meaty texture, are a versatile pantry staple. They add a satisfying heartiness to soups, salads, dips, and countless other dishes.

CRANBERRY BEANS

Cranberry beans, known for their beautiful speckled appearance that fades when cooked, offer a creamy texture and a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. These versatile beans are a favorite in Italian dishes, soups, and stews, bringing a delicious and visually interesting element to your meals.

CHICK PEAS

Chickpeas, also known as garbanzo beans, are a nutritional powerhouse packed with protein and fiber. Their nutty flavor and firm texture make them incredibly versatile, enjoyed in hummus, falafel, salads, curries, and countless other dishes around the world.

BLACK EYED PEAS

Black-eyed peas, sometimes called cowpeas, are a popular legume enjoyed worldwide. They offer a mild, slightly sweet flavor and a pleasing firm texture with a signature black eye in the center. These versatile beans are a staple in Southern cuisine and many other cultures, often enjoyed in stews, soups, and rice dishes for their delicious taste and symbolic association with good luck and prosperity.
