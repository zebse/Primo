PEANUTS

Peanuts are a type of legume that grow underground, producing pods that contain edible seeds inside. They are a popular snack food and cooking ingredient, and are a rich source of protein, healthy fats, and various vitamins and minerals.

LENTIL

Lentils are small, lens-shaped legumes that are inexpensive, highly nutritious, and commonly used in a variety of savory dishes. They come in different varieties based on color, size, and flavor, and are valued for their high protein, fiber, and mineral content.

WHITE BEANS

White beans, a versatile pantry staple, come in various shapes and sizes. They offer a mild, creamy texture and subtle flavor, making them perfect for soups, stews, dips, and creamy casseroles.

MUNG BEANS

Mung beans, also known as moong dal, are tiny, green lentils prized for their quick cooking time and subtle, slightly sweet flavor. They're a dietary staple in many cultures, enjoyed in soups, curries, and even sprouted as a nutritious addition to salads.

RED KIDNEY BEANS

Red kidney beans, named for their resemblance to a red kidney, are a powerhouse legume. They boast a hearty texture, earthy flavor, and rich nutritional profile, making them a delicious and healthy addition to chili, stews, and various international dishes.

BLACK KIDNEY BEANS

Black beans, boasting a deep, rich flavor and meaty texture, are a versatile pantry staple. They add a satisfying heartiness to soups, salads, dips, and countless other dishes.

CRANBERRY BEANS

Cranberry beans, known for their beautiful speckled appearance that fades when cooked, offer a creamy texture and a slightly sweet, nutty flavor. These versatile beans are a favorite in Italian dishes, soups, and stews, bringing a delicious and visually interesting element to your meals.

CHICK PEAS

Chickpeas, also known as garbanzo beans, are a nutritional powerhouse packed with protein and fiber. Their nutty flavor and firm texture make them incredibly versatile, enjoyed in hummus, falafel, salads, curries, and countless other dishes around the world.

BLACK EYED PEAS

Black-eyed peas, sometimes called cowpeas, are a popular legume enjoyed worldwide. They offer a mild, slightly sweet flavor and a pleasing firm texture with a signature black eye in the center. These versatile beans are a staple in Southern cuisine and many other cultures, often enjoyed in stews, soups, and rice dishes.

FLAXSEED/LINESEED

Flaxseed, also known as linseed, is a tiny but mighty seed packed with omega-3 fatty acids. This versatile crop can be enjoyed whole, ground into meal, or even pressed for its valuable oil, and is believed to offer a variety of health benefits.

ETHIOPIAN DESI CHICKPEAS

Ethiopian Desi Chickpeas are a type of legume that are native to Ethiopia. They are smaller and darker than their Kabuli counterparts and have a distinct nutty flavor. Desi chickpeas are often used in Ethiopian cuisine, adding a hearty and protein-packed element to dishes like stews, curries, and salads. They are also a popular ingredient in falafel and hummus.
